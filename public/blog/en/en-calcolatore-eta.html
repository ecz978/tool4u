The Mathematical Logic Behind the Calculation

Manual date subtraction consistently produces errors because human brains default to rounding months to thirty days. Real calendars operate irregularly, with twenty-eight, twenty-nine, thirty, and thirty-one day cycles that shift unpredictably across years. The Calcolatore Eta resolves this by implementing a sequential borrowing algorithm. It first isolates complete calendar years, then evaluates month boundaries. If the current day number falls below the birth day, the tool borrows days from the preceding month. If the current month index is lower than the birth month, it adjusts the year count and recalibrates. This automated logic eliminates manual mistakes and guarantees mathematically sound outputs.
