How the Pythagorean Theorem Works for Rectangles

The Pythagorean theorem states that in a right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of squares of the other two sides. A rectangle naturally contains right triangles when you draw its diagonal—the diagonal becomes the hypotenuse, while the width and height become the other two sides.

c² = a² + b²
where c is the diagonal, a is the width, and b is the height

This simple yet powerful mathematical relationship allows you to calculate any rectangle's diagonal with just two measurements. No complicated geometry knowledge required—just plug in your numbers and get instant results.

Practical Tips for Accurate Measurements

Ensure Measurement Consistency

Always measure both width and height in the same unit (inches, centimeters, feet, meters, etc.). The diagonal result will automatically use the same unit. Using different units will produce incorrect results.

Measure Accurately

Use a reliable measuring tool like a tape measure or ruler. For construction projects, precision matters—even small measurement errors compound when calculating diagonals. Measure at multiple points if the rectangle might not be perfectly uniform.

Double-Check Your Inputs

Before submitting your measurements to the calculator, verify both values are correct. A simple transposition error can significantly affect your diagonal calculation result.

Understand the Result

Remember that the diagonal is always longer than either the width or height individually. If your calculated diagonal is shorter than one of these measurements, something is wrong—recheck your inputs.

The Pythagorean theorem works for any rectangle, regardless of whether it's square or rectangular. As long as all corners form 90-degree angles, the formula applies perfectly.

Can I use this calculator for squares?

Absolutely! A square is a special rectangle where width equals height. Enter the same measurement for both dimensions, and you'll get the square's diagonal instantly.

What units can I use for measurements?

You can use any unit—millimeters, centimeters, meters, inches, feet, yards, etc. Just ensure both width and height use the same unit. The diagonal result will match your chosen unit.

Is the Pythagorean theorem the only way to calculate diagonal?

For rectangles with right angles, the Pythagorean theorem is the standard and most reliable method. It's the mathematical principle that defines rectangular geometry.
