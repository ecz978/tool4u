What Are Italian VAT Rates (IVA Aliquote)?

Italian VAT, known as IVA (Imposta sul Valore Aggiunto), is a consumption tax applied at different rates depending on the type of goods or services provided. Italy uses a hierarchical system with four main tax brackets, each serving different economic purposes.

Understanding these rates is crucial for accurate invoicing, tax declarations, and avoiding penalties. The rates range from 0% for certain exports to 22% for standard goods and services.

The Four Main Italian VAT Rates

VAT Rate Name Common Applications
0% Exempt / Export Rate International exports, certain services
4% Super-Reduced Rate Basic food items, books, medicines, medical equipment
10% Reduced Rate Hospitality, restaurant services, some publications
22% Standard Rate Most goods and services, electronics, apparel

How to Calculate IVA: Step-by-Step Guide

Basic Formula

The calculation is straightforward: Net Amount × VAT Rate = VAT Amount

Example: If you sell a product for €100 (excluding VAT) at the standard 22% rate:

  • VAT Amount: €100 × 22% = €22
  • Total Invoice: €100 + €22 = €122

Frequently Asked Questions About Italian VAT

What's the difference between IVA deductible and non-deductible?

IVA deductible refers to VAT you paid on business purchases that can be subtracted from VAT you collected from customers. Non-deductible VAT cannot be recovered and represents a cost to your business. Most business expenses generate deductible VAT, except for certain personal items and specific services like insurance.

Can I apply different VAT rates to different products in the same invoice?

Yes, absolutely. If you're selling items with different VAT rates (like a meal that includes both beverages and food), each item must be listed separately with its applicable rate. Our calculator supports multiple line items with different rates for comprehensive invoicing.

What happens if I charge the wrong IVA rate?

Charging incorrect VAT rates can result in penalties from the Italian Revenue Agency (Agenzia delle Entrate). It's crucial to apply the correct rate from the start. You can issue a corrective invoice if discovered, but prevention is better through proper calculation tools.

Is VAT included or excluded from the listed price in Italian stores?

In Italy, prices displayed in retail stores include VAT. However, in B2B transactions, businesses typically quote prices excluding VAT, with the tax added on invoices. Always clarify whether prices are net or gross when conducting business.

How often should I file VAT returns in Italy?

Italian businesses must file monthly VAT returns if their turnover exceeds €400,000, or quarterly returns below that threshold. Accurate record-keeping and regular calculations with our tool ensure you're always prepared for filing deadlines.
