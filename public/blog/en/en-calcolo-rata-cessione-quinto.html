What is a Fifth Assignment Payment?

A fifth assignment, known in Italian finance as "cessione del quinto," is a salary-backed loan where the borrower assigns one-fifth of their monthly income to the lender. This financial product is popular among employed individuals who need quick access to funds. The monthly payment is automatically deducted from the borrower's salary, making repayment convenient and reliable.

The name "fifth assignment" comes from the legal limit that restricts the maximum deduction to one-fifth of the borrower's gross monthly salary. This protection ensures that borrowers retain at least four-fifths of their income for living expenses.

Understanding the Key Variables

To accurately calculate your monthly payment, you need to consider several important factors:

  • Loan Amount: The total sum you wish to borrow
  • Interest Rate: The annual percentage rate charged by your lender
  • Loan Term: The number of months over which you'll repay the loan
  • Monthly Salary: Your gross monthly income (which determines the maximum fifth available)
  • Fees and Commissions: Additional costs imposed by the lender

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the maximum amount I can borrow with a fifth assignment?

The maximum loan amount depends on your gross monthly salary. Lenders typically allow you to borrow an amount that results in a monthly payment not exceeding one-fifth of your income. Our calculator helps you determine this maximum based on your salary and the interest rate offered.

How long does the application process take for a fifth assignment loan?

The application process is typically quick, often completed within 24 to 48 hours. Once approved, funds are usually transferred to your account within a few business days. The speed of processing may vary depending on your bank and the specific lender you choose.

Can I repay my fifth assignment loan early without penalties?

Many lenders allow early repayment without penalties, though this varies by lender and contract terms. Always review your specific loan agreement or contact your lender to understand any early repayment conditions. Early repayment can save you significant interest expense.

Is my credit history checked when applying for a fifth assignment loan?

Fifth assignment loans are salary-backed, meaning the lender has security through your salary deduction. While some lenders may perform a credit check, these loans are generally more accessible to people with lower credit scores compared to traditional unsecured loans.

What happens to my fifth assignment loan if I change jobs?

Changing jobs typically requires notifying your lender and potentially updating your assignment authorization with your new employer. Most contracts allow this transition, though procedures vary. Contact your lender immediately if you plan to change employment to avoid any complications.
