Key Factors That Determine Your Target Weight

Understanding the variables behind the calculation helps you interpret the results accurately. The algorithm prioritizes three fundamental inputs to ensure precision and reliability.

Biological Differences and Sex Distribution

Biological sex plays a significant role in metabolic rates, muscle mass potential, and natural fat distribution patterns. Men typically possess higher lean muscle mass and denser bone structures, naturally resulting in a higher optimal weight range. Women require essential fat stores for hormonal balance and reproductive health, which shifts their ideal baseline downward. The calculator automatically adjusts the baseline formulas to reflect these inherent physiological differences, ensuring fairness and accuracy across the board.

Height and Constitutional Frame Size

While height provides the primary vertical baseline, your constitutional frame type dictates actual skeletal width and density. Wrist circumference and joint size are reliable indirect indicators of this constitution. A person with a broad, heavy frame will naturally carry more muscle and bone mass than someone with a slender, light frame, even at the exact same height. Incorporating this variable prevents the common mistake of pushing a heavy-framed individual into an underweight category or overestimating the nutritional needs of a light-framed individual.
