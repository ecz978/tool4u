Understanding the Trapezoid Area Formula

The mathematical formula behind our calculator is straightforward: Area = ((Base1 + Base2) / 2) × Height

In practical terms, you add both parallel sides, divide by two to find the average, then multiply by the height. Our calculator performs these calculations automatically, eliminating manual computation errors. This formula works for all trapezoids regardless of whether they're right-angled, isosceles, or irregular.

What if my trapezoid isn't perfectly regular?

The formula works for all trapezoid types — right trapezoids, isosceles trapezoids, and irregular trapezoids. As long as two sides are parallel and you have accurate base and height measurements, the calculator provides the correct area regardless of the trapezoid's shape or angles.

How do I measure the height of a trapezoid?

The height is the perpendicular distance between the two parallel bases. Draw an imaginary line at a 90-degree angle from one base to the other. This perpendicular distance is your height. It doesn't follow the slanted sides — it goes straight up from the bottom base to the top base.
