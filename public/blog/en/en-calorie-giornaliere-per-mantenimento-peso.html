Understanding Calorie Needs for Maintaining Weight

Your daily calorie needs for maintaining weight are influenced by several factors, including your age, sex, weight, height, and activity level. When you consume more calories than your body needs, you gain weight. On the other hand, when you consume fewer calories than your body needs, you lose weight. To maintain your weight, you need to consume the same number of calories your body burns.

FAQs

Q: What is the difference between calorie needs for weight loss and maintaining weight?

A: Calorie needs for weight loss are typically lower than those for maintaining weight. When trying to lose weight, you need to consume fewer calories than your body burns to create a calorie deficit and promote weight loss. In contrast, when trying to maintain weight, you need to consume the same number of calories your body burns.
