What Is the Pythagorean Theorem?

The foundational principle governing right-angled triangles establishes a direct relationship between the three sides. According to classical geometry, the square of the longest edge equals the sum of the squares of the remaining two. Engineers and mathematicians represent this as a² + b² = c², where c denotes the side opposite the right angle, while a and b represent the adjacent legs. This rule applies exclusively to figures containing a precise 90-degree angle. Understanding this relationship remains critical across numerous disciplines, including carpentry, navigation, robotics, and computer graphics.
