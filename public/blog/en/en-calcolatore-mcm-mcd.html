Frequently Asked Questions

What is the primary difference between MCM and MCD?

The MCM (Least Common Multiple) determines the smallest shared multiple across all input numbers, while the MCD (Greatest Common Divisor) isolates the largest factor common to every entered value. One focuses on convergence upward, and the other on reduction downward.

Can this calculator handle three or more numbers simultaneously?

Yes. The algorithm processes multiple integers sequentially, identifying shared prime factors and unique components across the entire dataset to generate accurate composite results for any practical input size.

Why do MCM results sometimes appear unusually large?

When entered numbers share very few prime factors or are relatively prime to each other, the LCM expands rapidly. In extreme cases, the MCM will equal the direct product of the inputs, which is mathematically correct despite appearing disproportionate.
